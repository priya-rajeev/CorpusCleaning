Linguistics Research

Now we will ask you to clean a total of ten text samples. Each sample may be anywhere from just one sentence to a few sentences long. The samples will consist of a mix of Mandarin and English words.

For each text sample, we will ask you to separate the sample into sentences, as done in the previous example.

You can copy paste the text sample into the text area, then add in the vertical bars to segment the sentences.

Please carefully read the following rules:

  1. Whenever you think a sentence ends, please place double vertical lines (||) at the end of the sentence.

      For example: I walked to the store today. || I didn't buy anything, though. ||
  2. If there are any run-on sentences, please break them up to the best of your abilities.

      For example: Has anyone else looked at the class homework yet || I have not and I don't know how to do it || if anyone has any tips let me know! ||
  3. If the sentence is just a fragment, still place double vertical lines (||) at the end of the sentence.

      For example: Checking out a book ||
  4. Enclose all punctuation within the double vertical lines.

      For example: I am really excited about the weather today! || It's so nice outside... ||
  5. If there is a list of steps or bullet points, segment each bullet point into its own sentence.

      For example, the following list:
      Things to do this weekend:
        grocery shop
        take a hike
        go out to dinner

      would become
       Things to do this weekend: || grocery shop || take a hike || go out to dinner ||
  6. If there are any hyperlinks or email addresses, please include them in their own sentence (there should be a set of vertical bars before and after the link/email). Do this even if doing so will break up a sentence. If the email address and the vertical bars before and after were to be removed, the sentence should still be segmented correctly.

      For example: For concerns about the job posting at || google.com ||, please email the recruiter at || recruiter@recruiter.com ||
  7. If there are any emojis or emoticons, try to include them in whichever sentence seems closest to you.

      For example: I'm so happy :) || I ate some pancakes today! ||
  8. All in all, use your best judgement! There are no wrong or right answers :)
